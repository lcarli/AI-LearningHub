OutdoorGear Inc. - Frequently Asked Questions

BACKPACKS & DAYPACKS
====================

Q: What backpack size do I need for a weekend trip?
A: For a 2-3 night trip with lightweight gear: 40-55 liters. For 2-3 nights with standard 
gear: 55-70 liters. For 4+ nights: 65-80+ liters. The Osprey Atmos 65L (P006) is our most 
popular 3-season backpacking pack. For day hikes, the DayHiker 22L (P007) fits all essentials.
Rule of thumb: a full pack should weigh no more than 20% of your body weight.

Q: How do I fit a backpack correctly?
A: Fitting has three steps. Step 1: Measure your torso length (nape of neck to iliac crest 
on your hip). Most packs come in sizes S/M/L based on torso length — not body height. 
Step 2: Load the pack and put it on. The hipbelt padded sections should wrap around your 
hip bones, not your waist. Tighten the hipbelt first — 70% of pack weight should sit on 
your hips. Step 3: Tighten shoulder straps until they curve over your shoulders. Adjust 
load lifters so the pack top tilts slightly toward your head.

Q: What features should I look for in a backpacking pack?
A: Essential features: proper fit system (torso sizing), padded hip belt, top lid with 
pockets, front panel or side access to main compartment, hydration reservoir compatibility, 
attachment points for trekking poles and ice axe. Nice-to-have: rain cover, stretchy 
exterior pockets, removable top lid that converts to a daypack, aluminum frame stays.

Q: How do I pack a backpack efficiently?
A: Three layers: Bottom layer (sleeping bag, camp shoes — light and bulky items rarely 
needed during the day). Middle layer (clothing, food — heaviest items packed close to 
your back and as high as possible). Top layer (items needed during the day: first aid, 
rain gear, snacks, map). External pockets: water bottles, snacks, trekking poles.
